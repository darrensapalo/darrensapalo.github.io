What are Podcasts?

What are Podcasts?

You know how radio stations invite people to talk about things? They might have a doctor as a guest speaker so that they can talk about how to stay healthy, avoid Diabetes, and dealing with hypertension.

Now imagine that you can pick exactly what you want to listen to, instead of whatever is on the radio station.

That's what Podcasts are. They're pre-recorded and saved; You only need to find what you're interested in and download them.

How do I get Podcasts?

  1. Open the Google Playstore App.
  2. At the top is a white search bar; type "Podcast" and search.
  3. Download Podcast & Radio Addict, or you can choose whatever Podcast player to use. Think of it like a music player, but you can download music.
  4. Once downloaded, Allow any permissions required for the Podcast & Radio Addict. It will ask for permissions to access your files so that it can save the Podcasts in your phone.
  5. When you open the app, you can read some explanation of how to begin using Podcasts.
  6. Search whatever topic you're interested in (i.e. Computer Science).
  7. Look at all the search results and find something you want to try out.
  8. Once you've selected what to try, subscribe to the Podcast you selected. That way you'll know when they publish new episodes.
  9. Pick one of the earliest episodes to begin studying!
  10. There's three vertical dots on the side of the episode. Tap it to opens up the options, so you can download the episode to listen to it even offline!
  11. Once you're done downloading and listening to the episode, remember to delete the episode so you don't run out of space on your phone!

If that list of instructions confuses you, don't worry. I made a tutorial for How to download and use Podcasts with pictures.

Frequently Asked Questions

While you're using Podcasts, you might encounter these questions:

Will this consume a lot of data on my data plan?

Be warned! YES, this will consume a lot of data. It will use up battery on your device while you're downloading it too. If you're using a data plan that has a data cap (i.e. gives you only 300 MB or 900MB), be wary because you might end up not having enough data to use the Uber app.

Why does the download take so long?

These downloads take really long because they download 20 minutes to over 60 minutes of audio content. For example, I downloaded a 53 minute lecture on Computer Science which takes up 118.24MB. It's just something you have to wait for. You can try downloading them over night so they're ready for tomorrow.

The downloads don't finish. What do I do?

Sadly, our internet data services such as PLDT, Smart, and Globe aren't the best. Just try to resume the downloads when you can.

If things still don't work out, try finding something smaller to download.
